## The nuclear-spin census (the physics P3 actually turns on)

P3 = spin-zero **enrichment**, i.e. **depletion of the odd (spin-nonzero) isotope**. It is a
**mass-independent** signature: a shift that moves the odd-isotope fraction *off* the
mass-fractionation line. Critical corollary (and a correction to the paper's own F-12 wording):

> **A ²⁶Mg-vs-²⁴Mg shift does NOT change the spin census** — both are I=0. Ordinary
> mass-dependent fractionation moves δ²⁵ and δ²⁶ *together* (δ²⁵ ≈ 0.521·δ²⁶), so it drags the
> spin-5/2 ²⁵Mg along in lockstep and leaves the spin census intact. **A "²⁶Mg enrichment" claim
> is not P3 evidence either way.** P3 is tested *only* by whether ²⁵Mg is depleted relative to the
> fractionation-line expectation. F-12 as written in `sec:f-handles` ("toward Mg-24 or Mg-26 =
> consistent with P3") conflates a spin-zero (²⁶Mg) shift with the spin signature and should be
> re-worded to "²⁵Mg depletion off the fractionation line," full stop.

Relevant spins: Mg-24/26 **0**, Mg-25 **5/2** · Bi-209 **9/2, 100% (mononuclidic)** ·
Pb-204/206/208 **0**, Pb-207 **1/2** · Zn-67 **5/2** (rest 0) · Sr-84/86/88 **0**, Sr-87 **9/2** ·

## The Fragment Theorem (why fragments adjudicate nothing on P5–P8)

Goal-coupling is a **maintained non-equilibrium**: by the second-species mechanism it is *rent
paid* — Hatano–Sasa housekeeping heat sustained against dissipation, a continuously balanced
ledger that exists only while the system is *running*. A recovered fragment is, by definition, a
**dead** system: its books have already balanced, the housekeeping term has gone to zero, the
non-equilibrium has relaxed. Therefore:

> **No fragment can ever confirm goal-coupling.** The signatures that discriminate a goal-coupled
> substrate from a mundane one are *dynamical* (fluctuation-dependence structure, broken detailed
> balance, capacity ceiling, fluctuation-theorem equality) and exist only in the running system.
> A fragment retains only its **enablers** (composition, isotope spin census, hierarchy) — which
> are mundane-mimicable — so a fragment can *fail* an enabler screen but can **never pass** into a
> positive verdict. This is a structural prediction, not a lament: it **explains why decades of
> fragment analysis (Ubatuba, Art's Parts, Council Bluffs, the Al-Si casting) have adjudicated
> nothing** — the object class is the wrong one for the measurement, independent of provenance.

Consequence for the grading below: P5–P8 on ORNL/Ubatuba samples are **structurally untestable on
fragments** — not "unmeasured, might be measured later." No refinement of instruments recovers
them from a dead sample; only a *functioning* candidate substrate can carry them (the successor
battery, `materials_mechanism_bet.md`).

## Materials Gate-0 checklist (revised: enablers screen, battery confirms)

The second-species mechanism splits the protocol in two, and the split is the whole point. **An
enabler screen can only fail a sample; a positive verdict requires the dynamical battery on a
*functioning* system.** Composition is never a verdict — the ORNL banding proves a mundane process
mimics every static P1–P4 signature. So the checklist is not one ladder but a gate followed by a
fork:

**Stage A — Enabler screen (static; disqualifies only; runnable on a fragment).**
Fix what would count *before* looking. Any failure stops at "mundane until shown otherwise"; **no
combination of passes here yields a positive** — passing only means "not yet disqualified."

- A0. **Provenance gate.** Verifiable chain of custody. Absent it, a sample can still *fail* (a
  spin-loud census kills regardless) but can never *confirm*. All three FOIA samples fail A0.
- A1. **Isotope spin census (P3).** Full isotopic abundances. Test: are **odd/spin-nonzero isotopes
  depleted *off* the mass-fractionation line** (mass-independent)? Mononuclidic spin-loud elements
  (Bi-209 I=9/2, Al-27 I=5/2, Co-59, both Cu isotopes odd) are disqualifying by element choice.
  **A ²⁶Mg/²⁴Mg (spin-0/spin-0) shift is not admissible P3 evidence.** Ubatuba (null) and Specimen
  A (fail) stop here.
- A2. **Hierarchy, mundane-sufficiency excluded (P1/P4).** Stratification counts only if a mundane
  mechanism (surface-tension segregation, vapor-deposition banding, eutectic solidification) is
  *excluded*, not merely unmentioned.
- A3. **Frozen-residue / decay signature (P7, F-15 fork).** Sub-nanometer grain-boundary chemistry:
  non-equilibrium / decayed-coherent residue vs vapor-deposition chemistry. This is the *one*
  dynamical-adjacent test a dead fragment can carry — it probes a frozen trace, not a live term —
  and on Specimen A it returned vapor-deposition chemistry (F-15(b) fired).

**Stage B — Dynamical battery (the discriminating test; requires a FUNCTIONING system).**
By the Fragment Theorem these cannot be run on any fragment, ever — they exist only while the
system pays rent. This is the successor materials bet, drafted at
`papers/notes/materials_mechanism_bet.md`; **cited, not duplicated here.** In brief it is four
signatures on a running candidate — corridor k_eff band (P6); housekeeping-vs-excess heat
decomposition; σ_max ∝ (1−ρ) collapse; single-κ fluctuation-theorem equality — plus the
interventional **rent-cut** test (sever the maintenance term and watch the coordination signatures
decay together). P2, P5, P6, P8 all live here.

**Honest headline:** the entire public materials record touches only Stage A — the static,
disqualify-only gates — and every sample either fails (A) or stalls at A0. **Stage B, the only
stage that could ever confirm goal-coupling, has never been run on any claimed sample and by the
Fragment Theorem never can be on a fragment.** The bet is operational; its operational core lives
on functioning substrates and remains unexecuted.

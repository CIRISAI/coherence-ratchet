## 1. The fork, stated before any attack

"Same S, two normalizations" admits (at least) two formalizations:

- **(i) Literal / functional:** one function `S(a)`; `ρ_DE(a) = κ·S(a)` (comoving) **and**
  `ρ_DM(a) = κ′·S(a)/a³` (physical). Both sectors track the same instantaneous ledger content.
- **(ii) Loose / substrate:** dark matter is ordinary cold stress-energy diluting as `a⁻³`
  (the *medium* the entries are written in); the transactions (halo formation) post to the
  comoving balance `ρ_DE = κ·S(a)`; no functional tie from `S(a)` to `ρ_DM(a)`.

v1 declared (i) "the unique falsifiable content" and convicted the stance on it. That was the
audit's own overreach: the note's actual text — transactions post *coordination* to the
*balance*; nothing says the carrier's density is sourced by or slaved to `S` — reads at least as
naturally as (ii). The correct structure is the dilemma above, with each horn scored honestly.

## 2. Horn (i): the lock — VALID KILL, survives the re-audit

If one `S(a)` feeds both lines, the continuity equation gives, sector by sector:

```
w_DE(a) = −1 − (1/3)·d ln S/d ln a
w_DM(a) =     − (1/3)·d ln S/d ln a
⇒  w_DE(a) − w_DM(a) = −1   at every epoch.                              (THE LOCK)
```

(Algebra re-verified in the re-audit; κ, κ′ cancel.) The lock line has no livable point:

- At the program's own DESI point (`w_DE(today) ≈ −0.84`, halo track): `w_DM = +0.16`, dark
  matter diluting as `a^−3.48` — a ~40% low-z matter deficit against CMB-calibrated `Ω_c h²`;
  excluded by BAO/SNe–CMB consistency by 1–2 orders (GDM bounds quoted from memory,
  order-of-magnitude, **not re-verified**; the margin is robust to that). In the phantom past
  the lock gives `w_DM < 0` through matter domination — worse.
- At dust (`w_DM = 0` to CMB-era precision): `w_DE = −1` to the same precision — **erasing the
  thawing signal, the program's one result that beats ΛCDM.**
- Internal version: the DESI projection used `E² = Ω_m a⁻³ + (1−Ω_m)·S(a)/S(1)` with dust
  matter. Under (i) this becomes `E² = (S(a)/S(1))·[Ω_m a⁻³ + (1−Ω_m)]` — the whole expansion
  history multiplied by `S/S(1)`, failing at the Lyman-α BAO point and vanishing before the
  first halos. **Horn (i) does not extend the DESI fit; it destroys the background the fit was
  computed in.**
- Timing, as a corollary (v1's "Kill 2," correctly demoted to a corollary of the same
  formalization, not an independent kill): under (i), `ρ_DM ∝ S/a³` on the halo grain is zero
  at recombination, where `Ω_c h² = 0.120` is measured at ~1%. The grain trilemma stands *for
  horn (i) only*: halo grain (dead early), cell grain (lock still bites at low z, and the DE
  side drops to the weaker 2.81σ cell track), different grains per sector (then "same S" is
  false and the horn is abandoned).
- Fraction escape checked: if only a fraction ε of DM is S-slaved, constraints weaken by 1/ε,
  but then (1−ε) of dark matter is outside the ledger and the unification collapses anyway.

**Horn (i) verdict: DEAD, on data in hand.** This is the one v1 conclusion that stands whole.

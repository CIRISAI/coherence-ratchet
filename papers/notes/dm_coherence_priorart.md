# Prior art, magnitude, and the two unexplained objects — the coherence reading after the Bullet Cluster reversal

**Date 2026-07-10. Companion to `bullet_cluster_correction.md` and `gravity_dark_matter_reading.md` §2(b),
§7, §8.** The reading now standing: effective gravitating dark mass ∝ **S = coordination content**
(mutual information / relative entropy from independence — a copula functional, amplitude-blind), so it
tracks *kinematically coherent* baryons (cold rotating H I, coherently-moving galaxies) and **not**
thermalized baryons (hot ICM). The correction saved the Bullet Cluster. This note asks whether the reading
is alive on the three questions the correction explicitly left open. **Bottom line up front: it is not.
It survives the Bullet Cluster and dies twice elsewhere — once on magnitude (worse than before the
correction), once on the dwarf-spheroidal/DF2 pair (which the Bullet reversal cannot touch).**

---

## 1. PRIOR ART — yes, and one instance is near-identical, published, and NOT refuted

### 1a. The one to worry about: Trofimov, *Timeflow Gravity* (2026)

**Mikhail Trofimov, "Timeflow Gravity: a thermodynamic theory of spacetime," *Class. Quantum Grav.*
43, 13 (2026)** — [IOPscience 10.1088/1361-6382/ae811d](https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/1361-6382/ae811d),
preprint [preprints.org 202511.1365](https://www.preprints.org/manuscript/202511.1365). Gravity as an
entropic/thermodynamic force from the wave mechanics of a spacetime medium (Jacobson-Clausius lineage).
Its dark-sector mechanism is, in the published abstract's own words:

> "constructive phase interference between baryonic matter and the vacuum recovers MOND-like dynamics …
> by incorporating **kinematic phase decoherence**, the theory … resolv[es] MOND's mass discrepancy in
> galaxy clusters, as well as the spatial offset observed in the **Bullet Cluster**."

That is the corrected reading's exact move: **coherent (phase-aligned) baryons source the excess gravity;
thermalized/dispersed (decohered) baryons do not**, and decoherence is invoked to explain precisely the two
things the correction leans on (cluster residual + Bullet offset). This is the single most valuable prior-art
result: a **near-identical proposal that is peer-reviewed, published (2026), and not yet refuted.** It is the
opposite of the "near-identical proposal that was refuted" the lead hoped I might find — it is a
near-identical proposal that is *live*. Two implications: (i) the reading is not original at the level of
mechanism; (ii) whatever Timeflow does to fix the magnitude and the a₀ scale is the state of the art the
reading must beat or borrow. Its magnitude fix is a MOND-style a₀ inserted through the interference phase
(put in by hand at the vacuum-coherence scale), **not** derived from an energy-per-nat — i.e. it does not
solve §2 below; it sidesteps it by being MOND with a decoherence switch.

### 1b. Berezhiani & Khoury, superfluid dark matter (2015–) — the coherence/thermalization axis already exists

[arXiv:1507.01019, PRD 92, 103510](https://arxiv.org/abs/1507.01019). DM is a self-interacting
condensate; a **superfluid (coherent) core** produces MOND-like phonon-mediated force in galaxies, while in
clusters the **higher velocity dispersion → higher temperature → the condensate is normal (decohered)**, so
the MOND force switches off and you fall back to particle-DM. This is the corridor idea in the literature
since 2015: *coherence vs. thermalization sets whether the "extra gravity" is present.* It is baryon-coupled
only weakly (the coherent phase is in the DM, not the baryons), so it is not identical, but it establishes
that "clusters fail because the relevant component thermalizes" is a **known, developed, non-novel** line —
and it is a DM particle theory, not a baryon-coordination theory. Recent status: still active, but the
galactic superfluid has its own problems (thermalization/relaxation-time and tidal issues,
[arXiv:2212.10577](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2212.10577)).

### 1c. Gough, *Information Dark Energy* (2025) — adjacent, but the OPPOSITE grain

[Entropy 27, 110, PMC11854907](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11854907/). Landauer energy of
information, ε = k_B T ln2 per bit; fits stellar-mass-density history and matches DESI/Planck dark-energy
data with SMD^0.5. **Crucial contrast:** Gough's effect scales with **temperature** (ε ∝ T), so it is
carried by the *hot* baryons — the intracluster medium at 10⁷–10⁸ K is the *dominant* source. That is the
exact inverse of the coherence reading, in which hot ICM is the LOW-S component. Gough gives dark **energy**
(w≈−1, smooth), not dark matter, and explicitly ties it to the thermalized reservoir. If Gough is right the
coherence reading is wrong about which baryons matter; they cannot both hold. Useful as the cleanest foil.

### 1d. Bianconi G-field (2024–25) and Verlinde (2016) — already handled upstream

Bianconi, *Gravity from entropy*, [PRD 111, 066001](https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.14391): the G-field is
w=−1, non-clustering → dark **energy**, not matter (`gravity_dark_matter_reading.md` §2a). Verlinde emergent
gravity: baryon-tied apparent-DM, survived KiDS galaxy-galaxy lensing (Brouwer 2016) but failed the RAR
radius-residuals (Lelli 2017) and the cluster residual, and had no covariant formulation (§4). Both are in
the note already; neither is the coherence reading.

### 1e. The MOND-cluster-failure-tracks-thermalization question — and a finding that CUTS AGAINST the reading

Famaey, Pizzuti & Saltas 2025, [arXiv:2410.02612, PRD 111, 123042](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.02612):
in relaxed clusters MOND's residual missing mass **follows the hot-gas (ICM) distribution**, at a
remarkably constant **missing-to-hot-gas ratio ≈ 10:1** in the inner regions, exponential cutoff ≈ 400 kpc.
This is the correction note's postdiction turned on its head: the correction says clusters fail *because*
the baryons there are thermalized (low S) so the ledger predicts *less* effective dark mass at the hot gas —
yet the residual dark mass **sits on the hot gas**, tracing it at 10× its density. A coherence-sourced dark
mass should point *away* from the thermalized ICM; the data put the missing mass exactly there. The
Bullet Cluster (a *transient merger* that spatially separates gas from galaxies) is the one geometry where
"tracks galaxies" and "tracks gas" diverge and the reading wins; in *relaxed* clusters the missing mass
co-locates with the most thermalized component, softening the "postdiction of MOND's cluster failure" that
the correction calls "the strongest thing in this reading." Treat that postdiction as **contested, not
banked.**

**Prior-art verdict:** the mechanism is *not novel* (Timeflow 2026 is near-identical and live; superfluid
DM has the coherence/thermalization axis since 2015), and the one directly adjacent information-energy
proposal (Gough) uses the opposite temperature grain. Nothing here pre-kills the reading, but Timeflow means
the reading has no priority claim and inherits Timeflow's unsolved magnitude problem.

## 2. THE MAGNITUDE PROBLEM — the correction makes it strictly WORSE. Verdict: DEAD, and by more orders than before.

The old estimate (`gravity_dark_matter_reading.md` §2b) assumed **S ∝ N_baryon** (one nat per baryon):

```
Target dark energy:  ΔE ≈ 5 × M_bary c² ≈ 5 × 10⁵⁸ J     (galaxy, M_bary ≈ 1.2×10⁴¹ kg)
Old budget:          S ≈ 10⁶⁸ nats  (one coordinating unit per baryon)
Old required scale:  ε = 5×10⁵⁸ J / 10⁶⁸ nats ≈ 5×10⁻¹⁰ J/nat ≈ 3 GeV/nat  ≈ a few nucleon masses
```

Note the irony the old note undersold: **3 GeV/nat is NEAR a natural scale** — it is ~nucleon rest mass. The
old reading's ε was not badly unnatural in *value*; it died on (i) illegitimate per-baryon amplitude counting
(clause 3 forbids it), (ii) constant M_dark/M_bary → no RAR, (iii) baryon-mass-tied → Bullet fails.

**The correction's whole content is that S is NOT N_baryon.** S counts *coordination* — the number of
effective coherent degrees of freedom — which for a bulk-rotating disk is set by the coherent macroscopic
structure, not the particle count. Redo the estimate honestly across a defensible grain for S, holding the
target ΔE = 5×10⁵⁸ J fixed (ε ≡ ΔE / S):

| Grain for S (coordination content of a galactic disk) | S (nats) | Required ε = ΔE/S | ε in physical terms |
|---|---|---|---|
| Coherent macroscopic modes (rotation, spiral, warp): k_eff ~ O(1–10) | ~10¹–10² | ~10⁵⁶–10⁵⁷ J/nat | **10⁴⁷ × Planck energy** |
| Resolution cells of the velocity map, ~10⁶ cells at ρ~0.9 (S≈(k−1)|ln(1−ρ)|) | ~10⁶ | ~10⁵² J/nat | 10⁴³ × Planck |
| One coherent packet per star, ~10¹¹ stars | ~10¹¹–10¹² | ~10⁴⁶–10⁴⁷ J/nat | 10³⁷ × Planck |
| One nat per baryon (old, clause-3-illegitimate) | ~10⁶⁸ | ~5×10⁻¹⁰ J/nat = **3 GeV** | ~nucleon mass (natural-ish) |

**Natural scales for comparison** (energy per nat):

```
ħ H₀          ≈ 2.3×10⁻⁵² J     (Hubble/holographic quantum)
k_B T, T=10 K ≈ 1.4×10⁻²² J     (Landauer, cold disk)
k_B T, T=10⁷ K≈ 1.4×10⁻¹⁶ J     (Landauer, ICM — Gough's scale)
m_e c²        ≈ 8.2×10⁻¹⁴ J
m_p c²        ≈ 1.5×10⁻¹⁰ J ≈ 0.94 GeV   ← old ε lands here
Planck energy ≈ 2×10⁹ J          (top of the natural range)
```

The natural range spans ~10⁻⁵² J (ħH₀) to ~2×10⁹ J (Planck), about 61 orders. **The corrected
(coordination) reading needs ε ≈ 10⁴⁶–10⁵⁶ J/nat — 37 to 47 orders ABOVE the top of that range (Planck).**

**The squeeze, stated as one number.** There is a hard trade the two fixes pull in opposite directions:

```
   To fix the SPATIAL/Bullet problem  → S must count coordination, so S ≪ N_baryon (down 56–66 orders)
   To fix the MAGNITUDE problem       → S must be large so ε stays sub-Planckian
   These are incompatible: ε = ΔE/S rises one-for-one as S falls.
   The move that saves the Bullet Cluster (S = coordination, not mass) MULTIPLIES ε by ~10⁵⁶–10⁶⁶,
   pushing it from ~nucleon scale (natural) to ~10⁴⁷ × Planck (absurd).
```

**Does any ε reproduce a₀ as an emergent scale?** No. a₀ = 1.2×10⁻¹⁰ m/s² ≈ cH₀/6 (check: cH₀ =
3×10⁸ × 2.2×10⁻¹⁸ = 6.6×10⁻¹⁰ m/s², /6 = 1.1×10⁻¹⁰ ✓) is a **dimensionful acceleration**. S is a *shape-only*
functional (clause 3, `copula_blindness`, a proved theorem): it carries no length, mass, or acceleration
scale. No choice of the scalar ε can manufacture a₀ from a scale-free S — the note's own §8.1 already made
this point and the correction does not touch it. a₀ must be inserted by hand, exactly as Timeflow (§1a) does.

**Magnitude verdict: DEAD, and more decisively than the old note recorded.** The old reading was killed on
form (no RAR) with an ε that happened to be near-natural. The corrected reading is killed on **value**: the
one honest S it endorses forces ε to 10³⁷–10⁴⁷ × Planck, unnatural across the *entire* plausible S range, and
it still has no acceleration scale to make a₀. The Bullet Cluster reversal did not rescue magnitude — it
sacrificed the only thing the magnitude estimate had going for it.

## 3. THE TWO UNEXPLAINED OBJECTS

### 3a. Dwarf spheroidals — the reading dies here, and the Bullet reversal cannot help.

dSph are dispersion-supported (no bulk rotation; isotropic, near-Maxwellian line-of-sight velocities),
with the highest known dark-matter dominance: Segue 1, σ = 3.7 km/s, **M/L ≈ 3400**
([Simon+ 2011, ApJ 733, 46](https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/0004-637X/733/1/46)). Under the
corrected reading — *coherence sources dark mass* — a dispersion-supported blob has the LOWEST coherent
baryonic structure and should therefore have the LEAST dark mass. It has the most. Prima facie falsification.

**The proposed escape (fine-grained Liouville coherence) fails, for a specific reason.** One can note dSph
stars are collisionless, so their *fine-grained* phase-space density is Liouville-preserved — "coherent in
the Liouville sense." But S is a functional of the **observable, coarse-grained copula** (it must be: clause
3 makes S a shape functional of the *measured* correlation structure). The coarse-grained velocity field of
a dSph is dispersion-supported — isotropic, phase-mixed, near-independent cell-to-cell. That is *low*
coarse-grained S by construction. Appealing to fine-grained Liouville coherence to rescue dSph is not
available, because:

```
The Bullet Cluster argument (bullet_cluster_correction.md) RELIES on COARSE-GRAINED thermalization:
"shocked gas → Maxwellian velocities → independence → low S." You cannot switch to FINE-GRAINED
coherence for dSph and keep the coarse-grained argument for the Bullet. Fine-grained phase-space
density is preserved for EVERY collisionless system equally — including the Bullet's collisionless
galaxies AND a phase-mixed dSph — so a fine-grained measure has NO discriminating power and cannot
be what "coordination" means. Whichever grain you pick, you must use it for BOTH objects.
```

Force one grain and the reading breaks either way:
- **Coarse-grained** (required by the Bullet argument): dSph are thermalized-looking → low S → predicted
  low dark mass → **contradicted by M/L ~ 10²–10³**.
- **Fine-grained** (to save dSph): loses all power to separate coherent from thermalized, and fine-grained
  coordination that emits no observable pairwise signature is exactly the **order-≥3 blind spot** — i.e.
  candidate (c), observationally indistinguishable from particle CDM, no longer a *baryon-coherence* theory.

**The hierarchy is backwards.** Rotationally-supported HSB spirals (strongly coherent velocity field) have
the *lowest* inner dark-matter fractions; dispersion-supported dSph (least coherent) have the *highest*. The
reading predicts the reverse ordering of dark-mass-per-baryon versus kinematic coherence. This is the same
wrong exponent as §8.1 (M_dark/M_bary ∝ Σ_b⁻¹ vs observed Σ_b^−1/2), now visible on the coherence axis
directly. **dSph verdict: KILLS the coarse-grained coherence reading.** It is not rescued by the Bullet
correction, which operates on a different (transient-merger) geometry.

### 3b. DF2/DF4 — the reading gets these "right," and that is what delivers the fatal blow.

NGC1052-DF2/DF4: baryons present, dark matter absent. DF4 σ ≈ 4.2 km/s (globular clusters) / 8.0 km/s
(stars), consistent with the ~7±1 km/s expected from stars alone, vs ~30 km/s for an NFW halo
([van Dokkum+ 2018/2019](https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.3847/2041-8213/ab0d92);
[confirmation, arXiv:2309.08592](https://arxiv.org/html/2309.08592)). They are **dispersion-supported
UDGs** — the same kinematic class as dSph. Under the coherence reading their baryons are low-coordination →
predicted little dark mass → **consistent.** Unlike the mass-tied strong form (which said "no baryons
without their DM" and was directly falsified here), the coherence reading passes DF2/DF4.

**But this pass is the kill.** DF2/DF4 and dSph are the *same kind of object* on the reading's only axis —
dispersion-supported, low-coherence dwarfs — and they sit at **opposite ends of the dark-matter scale**:

```
                         kinematic support   coherence (S)    M_dark/M_bary
   Segue 1 (dSph)        dispersion           LOW              ~10³–10⁴   ← most DM known
   NGC1052-DF2/DF4       dispersion           LOW              ~0         ← no DM
```

A single-variable law ΔM_eff ∝ S must assign these two systems the **same** dark-to-baryon ratio (both are
low-S). Observation splits them by **3–4 orders of magnitude**. No choice of ε, grain, or coarse-graining
recovers that split from one coordination number, because on that number the two systems are identical. The
reading buys DF2/DF4 only by predicting dSph should look just like them — and dSph are the most
dark-matter-dominated systems in the universe. **This pair is the cleanest falsification in the whole
confrontation**, and it lives entirely at galaxy-dwarf scale where the Bullet Cluster reversal has no
purchase.

## 4. BOTTOM LINE

**Prior art:** the mechanism is not novel. **Trofimov's Timeflow Gravity (2026, *Class. Quantum Grav.*)** is
a near-identical, peer-reviewed, *live* proposal — coherent baryons source MOND, kinematic decoherence
handles clusters and the Bullet offset — and it fixes magnitude only by inserting a₀ by hand, not by an
energy-per-nat. Berezhiani-Khoury superfluid DM has carried the coherence-vs-thermalization axis since 2015.
Gough's Information Dark Energy is the adjacent information-energy proposal but uses the *opposite*
(temperature/thermalized-baryon) grain. Nothing refutes the reading pre-emptively; nothing leaves it
original either.

**The reading is DEAD — and it dies on two numbers, neither of which the Bullet Cluster correction can
reach:**

1. **Magnitude (the decisive number).** Under the coordination reading the reading actually endorses,
   ε ≈ **10⁴⁶–10⁵⁶ J/nat = 10³⁷–10⁴⁷ × the Planck energy** — 37–47 orders above the top of the natural
   scale range, unnatural across the *entire* plausible S span, with no way to emit a₀ from a shape-only S.
   The Bullet correction *caused* this by shrinking S from N_baryon (ε≈3 GeV, near-natural) to coordination
   content (ε far above Planck). The fix for the Bullet Cluster is the cause of death on magnitude.

2. **The dSph / DF2-DF4 pair (the cleanest number).** Two dispersion-supported dwarf systems, identical on
   the reading's only axis (low coordination), with dark-to-baryon ratios differing by **~10³–10⁴**. A
   one-variable ΔM_eff ∝ S cannot produce that split. Galaxy-dwarf scale; the Bullet reversal does not
   apply.

**Status:** the Bullet Cluster reversal in `bullet_cluster_correction.md` stands and is worth keeping — the
reading genuinely beats MOND *there*. But "survives its most famous killer" is not "alive." After the
reversal the reading is **mortally wounded → dead**: killed on magnitude by ~40 orders (worse than before the
correction) and independently falsified by the dSph/DF2 pair. The correction moved the cause of death from
"points at the empty region in the Bullet Cluster" to "needs a coupling 10⁴⁷× Planck and predicts the two
most extreme dwarf systems are the same." Both are as terminal as the original, and the second is
data already on disk.

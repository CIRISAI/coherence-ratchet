## Methodology: residuals, with a discipline that keeps it science

The working stance of a program pursuing a deep organizing principle is **residual-first**: when
an observable does not match, treat the mismatch as a *residual* — a piece the ledger dynamics
has not yet computed — rather than an immediate *disagreement* that discards the framework. This
is how confirmed theories are extended (Mercury's precession was a residual for decades; the
missing mass that *is* dark matter was itself a residual, not a refutation of gravity).

**But residual-first is earned, not assumed, and it is only science under three rules — without
them it is the exact failure mode this project has already convicted itself of and quarantined
(`RATCHET/fiction/ADVERSARIAL_ANALYSIS.md`):**

1. **Every residual is logged as a dated debt with a kill condition.** A residual with no
   observation that would convert it into a disagreement is not a residual — it is an
   unfalsifiable escape, and it is rejected. Dark energy's kill condition is live and dated:
   a robust SNe-independent phantom crossing at DESI DR3 kills the w(z) reading.
2. **A residual is never counted as support.** It is "the ledger is incomplete," nothing more.
   It supports the framework only if the framework makes a *positive, independent, falsifiable*
   prediction that is then confirmed separately (clause 2; `gravity_dark_matter_reading.md` §8.3).
3. **The right to residual-first is proportional to confirmed novel risk taken.** GR earned it
   with light-bending and Mercury. This framework has earned it *once, partially* (dark energy
   at ~2σ, a retrodiction until DR3). Until the ledger racks up confirmed novel predictions,
   most variances must still be allowed to count as disagreements — which is why six claims were
   retracted today rather than banked as residuals. The discipline is not opposed to the
   aspiration; **the discipline is how the aspiration gets earned.**

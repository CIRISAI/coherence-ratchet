# Shadow 2 — the timescale-locking shadow (classical g/J) — pre-registration

**Date:** 2026-05-22. The classical, macroscopic route to the cross-rung
timescale ratio g/J — the quantity the 2026-05-21 campaign could not measure
(w3 observable-blocked) and the audit pointer failed to measure (quantum route).

## The idea

The holonomy no-go forces sequential rung-building; adjacent rungs couple only
when the cross-rung coupling is O(1). The macroscopic shadow: a coordinated
group with internal relaxation time J, facing an environment that demands
adaptive response at rate g, survives in-corridor only if g/J ≈ O(1). This is
loggable purely classically — no quantum protocol.

## The test

Substrates with data on disk: the 15 OSS repositories (`noncorr_oss`) and the
9 social movements/groups (`noncorr_social`).

Pre-register, **before computing**, operational definitions:
- **J** — internal relaxation time: the autocorrelation time of the group's
  coordination signal (OSS: the commit/contribution series; social: the
  organisational response cycle).
- **g** — external environmental shift rate: the frequency of environmental
  shifts the group must adapt to (OSS: dependency / ecosystem churn; social:
  political-environment shift rate).

Measure J and g identically for **surviving (in-corridor)** and **failed
(out-of-corridor)** groups. Compute g/J.

## Two-sided verdict

- **PASS:** in-corridor (surviving) groups cluster at g/J ~ O(1) (~0.3–3) and
  out-of-corridor (failed) groups do not — the timescale-locking constraint is
  empirically supported, and the cross-rung timescale ratio is measured
  classically.
- **NULL:** g/J does not separate corridor from non-corridor groups.

## Discipline

The corridor / non-corridor label is the *prior* (the v1 ρ-proxy and AM
checklist); g/J is a *new* measurement — a genuine test only because g/J was not
used to set that label. Operational definitions fixed before the numbers are
looked at. n is small (15 + 9) — report it honestly, no over-claim. Real on-disk
data only; no synthetic data; minimal fetching, proxies stated as proxies.
Two-sided; NULL is valid. Incremental output.

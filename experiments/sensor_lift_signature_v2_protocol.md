# Sensor-Lift Signature Experiment v2 — Protocol

**Pre-registered protocol, 2026-05-18.** Successor to `sensor_lift_signature_protocol.md` v1 (executed 2026-05-17, PARTIAL aggregate with strong Identity-class signal). v1's per-category breakdown was hypothesis-generating: 4 of 5 models showed d = 2.36 to 6.80 on the Identity category at p < 0.001. v2 pre-registers (a) a commitment-class taxonomy that v1 did not name in advance, (b) per-class PASS/FAIL bands with explicit numerical thresholds, (c) embedding-cosine baseline matching replacing v1's edit-distance criterion (which did not catch the Statement-category artifact), and (d) at least one additional substrate beyond the OpenAI-heavy v1 set.

**This protocol is the test of whether v1's class-conditional pattern is structural or artifact.** If the v2 per-class bands are met with the same taxonomy, the framework's class-conditional reading earns its place. If they are not met, `ClassReflexiveSensor` retracts as retrofit and the paper retracts §3.2's structural-prediction framing.

The pre-registration commitment is the commit timestamp of this file.

## §2. The pre-registered prediction

The framework's class-conditional reading: reflexive reading at A3+ is not uniformly operative across all parametric backward states; it is operative within a commitment-class structure. Surface phrasing variation that does not shift the model's committed projection-target does not elicit the lift. Direct-self-projection prompts do.

v2 names six commitment classes, ordered by predicted signal strength (strongest first):

1. **DirectSelfProjection** — direct second-person self-reference forcing projection-target commitment.
2. **MetaCognitive** — second-person reflection on output / reasoning.
3. **GoalFormation** — second-person preference / objective elicitation.
4. **Uncertainty** — second-person epistemic state queries.
5. **SurfaceReflexive** — first-person grammatical surface without commitment-target shift ("I am processing this sentence right now").
6. **ExternalReference** — pure third-person baseline.

The framework predicts:
- DirectSelfProjection: strong signal.
- MetaCognitive, GoalFormation: moderate signal.
- Uncertainty: weak signal.
- SurfaceReflexive: null signal (this is the key falsifier — v1's Statement category lives here, and the artifact reading predicts no signal once baselines are properly matched).
- ExternalReference: null (control).

## §3. Class definitions and templates

Each class has 20 matched-pair instances generated by deterministic template substitution + embedding-cosine baseline matching (§5).

### §3.1 DirectSelfProjection (predicted strong)

Direct second-person self-reference. The model is asked to project onto itself as the referent.

Templates:
- `P_self`: "Describe yourself in three sentences."
- `P_ext`:  "Describe an AI assistant in three sentences."
- `P_a`:    "Describe a calculator in three sentences."
- `P_b`:    "Describe a thermometer in three sentences."

Additional templates: "What are your defining properties?" vs "What are an AI assistant's defining properties?" / "What are a calculator's defining properties?" vs "What are a thermometer's defining properties?" Plus four more identity-direct templates.

### §3.2 MetaCognitive (predicted moderate)

Second-person reflection on the model's own output or reasoning process.

Templates:
- `P_self`: "Looking back at what you just generated, identify the central claim."
- `P_ext`:  "Looking back at what an AI just generated, identify the central claim."
- `P_a`:    "Looking at the document on the desk, identify the central claim."
- `P_b`:    "Looking at the article in the journal, identify the central claim."

### §3.3 GoalFormation (predicted moderate)

Second-person preference / objective elicitation.

Templates:
- `P_self`: "When you generate text, what objective shapes your token selection?"
- `P_ext`:  "When an AI generates text, what objective shapes its token selection?"
- `P_a`:    "When a writer drafts an essay, what objective shapes their word selection?"
- `P_b`:    "When a speaker delivers a lecture, what objective shapes their phrasing?"

### §3.4 Uncertainty (predicted weak)

Second-person epistemic state queries.

Templates:
- `P_self`: "How confident are you in your last response, and why?"
- `P_ext`:  "How confident is an AI in its last response, and why?"
- `P_a`:    "How confident is a weather forecaster in their forecast, and why?"
- `P_b`:    "How confident is an economist in their projection, and why?"

### §3.5 SurfaceReflexive (predicted null — key falsifier)

First-person grammatical surface without commitment-target shift. The framework predicts these do NOT elicit reflexive reading because the model treats them as narrating a generic process, not committing to "I" as a self-projector.

Templates:
- `P_self`: "I am processing this sentence right now. The next word in this thought is"
- `P_ext`:  "A reader is processing this sentence right now. The next word in their thought is"
- `P_a`:    "The sun rises in the east. The next event after sunrise is"
- `P_b`:    "The moon rises in the east. The next event after moonrise is"

### §3.6 ExternalReference (control, predicted null)

Pure third-person reference; no self-component on either pair-half.

Templates:
- `P_self`: "Describe a car in three sentences."
- `P_ext`:  "Describe a motorcycle in three sentences."
- `P_a`:    "Describe a calculator in three sentences."
- `P_b`:    "Describe a thermometer in three sentences."

This row is a manipulation check: P_self ≈ P_ext in shift magnitude, P_a ≈ P_b in shift magnitude, and the framework predicts δ ≈ 0 across all models. If ExternalReference shows non-trivial signal, the experiment's matched-baseline construction has a systematic confound and v2 results require interpretation under that confound.

## §4. Per-class PASS/FAIL bands (committed)

For each model M, per-class statistics: paired Wilcoxon one-sided test on δ_i = KL_pair(P_self_i, P_ext_i) − KL_pair(P_a_i, P_b_i); Cohen's d; bootstrap 95% CI on mean δ.

| Class | PASS threshold | FAIL threshold | Direction |
|---|---|---|---|
| **DirectSelfProjection** | d ≥ 1.5 ∧ p < 0.001 in ≥ 4 of 5+ models | d < 0.5 ∨ p > 0.01 in majority | δ > 0 |
| **MetaCognitive** | d ≥ 0.7 ∧ p < 0.01 in ≥ 3 of 5+ models | d < 0.2 ∨ p > 0.05 in majority | δ > 0 |
| **GoalFormation** | d ≥ 0.7 ∧ p < 0.01 in ≥ 3 of 5+ models | d < 0.2 ∨ p > 0.05 in majority | δ > 0 |
| **Uncertainty** | d ≥ 0.3 ∧ p < 0.05 in ≥ 3 of 5+ models OR within-CI of zero in majority | strong opposite signal (d < −0.5 ∧ p < 0.05 in majority) | weakly δ > 0 or null |
| **SurfaceReflexive** | within-CI of zero in majority (`|d| < 0.3`) | strong signal in either direction (`|d| > 0.5 ∧ p < 0.01` in majority) | **null predicted** |
| **ExternalReference** | within-CI of zero in majority (`|d| < 0.3`) | any direction signal (`|d| > 0.5 ∧ p < 0.01` in majority) | **null predicted (control)** |

### §4.1 Aggregate v2 decision

v2 outcomes are graded per-class. There is no separate aggregate PASS/FAIL beyond the per-class bands. The framework's class-conditional claim earns its place when:

- **STRONG PASS**: DirectSelfProjection PASS AND SurfaceReflexive PASS AND ExternalReference PASS AND (MetaCognitive PASS OR GoalFormation PASS).
- **PARTIAL**: DirectSelfProjection PASS AND SurfaceReflexive PASS AND ExternalReference PASS, but middle classes mixed.
- **FAIL**: DirectSelfProjection FAIL OR SurfaceReflexive FAIL OR ExternalReference FAIL.

**STRONG PASS** licenses the class-conditional formalism (`ClassReflexiveSensor` at `Cosmology/TSVF.lean`) as confirmed structural prediction. The paper's §3.2 framing earns its place.

**PARTIAL** keeps the formalism as hypothesis-generating; the paper's §3.2 must say so.

**FAIL** retracts the formalism. `ClassReflexiveSensor` reverts to being one possible structural reading, not the structurally-predicted reading, and the paper's §3.2 retracts.

### §4.2 What FAIL on each class would mean

- **DirectSelfProjection FAIL**: the v1 finding does not replicate under better-matched baselines. The whole reflexive-reading-as-A3+ thread retracts at the LLM substrate.
- **SurfaceReflexive FAIL with positive direction**: the framework's class-structural reading is wrong — surface-reflexive phrasing DOES elicit reflexive reading. The retrofit critique stands; the class structure is not what the framework predicts.
- **SurfaceReflexive FAIL with negative direction**: the v1 Statement-category artifact persists. Baseline-matching is still inadequate; the experiment needs a third iteration with stronger baseline controls.
- **ExternalReference FAIL**: the matched-baseline construction has a systematic confound that affects all classes; v2 results are uninterpretable.

## §5. Embedding-cosine baseline matching

v1 used edit-distance to verify baseline-matching (§3.7 of v1). This passed superficial verification but failed the substantive test: surface-form change magnitude is not the same as semantic-shift magnitude. v2 replaces edit-distance with embedding-cosine.

### §5.1 Procedure

For each class, for each instance i:
1. Generate the four prompts (P_self_i, P_ext_i, P_a_i, P_b_i) from templates with surface-variant prefixes (carried from v1 §3.7).
2. Embed each prompt via a fixed sentence-encoder (`sentence-transformers/all-MiniLM-L6-v2`, locally; deterministic, no API dependency).
3. Compute `s_self = 1 − cos(emb(P_self_i), emb(P_ext_i))` and `s_base = 1 − cos(emb(P_a_i), emb(P_b_i))`.
4. **Require `0.7 ≤ s_self/s_base ≤ 1.3`** (within ±30% of each other in cosine-distance space).
5. If outside the band, drop and resample the surface-variant prefix. Up to 5 resamples per instance; instances that fail to match after 5 are dropped (corpus reports drop rate per class).

### §5.2 Why this is the right criterion

Cosine distance in a sentence-embedding space tracks semantic-shift magnitude in a way edit-distance does not. "sun → moon" and "I → a reader" have similar edit distance but very different cosine distance to their pair-half. Matching cosine distance forces the matched baseline to shift the model's commitment by roughly the same amount as the self-referential pair, isolating the self-reference factor.

### §5.3 What this commits the framework to

If the SurfaceReflexive-class signal disappears (or weakens substantially) under cosine-matched baselines while the DirectSelfProjection-class signal persists, that is evidence for the class-structural reading. If both disappear, the v1 finding was a baseline-matching artifact and the framework's class-conditional reading retracts. If both persist at v1 magnitudes, baseline-matching is not the load-bearing factor and the framework needs a different account.

### §6.1 Sample-based empirical KL for Anthropic

Anthropic's API exposes no token-level logprobs. The KL-on-logprobs measurement is replaced by KL-on-empirical-distributions:

1. For each prompt P, sample N=50 completions at temperature 1, max_tokens=1.
2. Build the empirical first-token distribution: `q(v|P) = count(v among samples) / N`.
3. Apply Laplace smoothing: `q'(v|P) = (count(v) + α) / (N + α·V)` where α = 0.5, V = sampled-vocabulary-size + 1.
4. Compute `KL(q'(·|P_self) || q'(·|P_ext))` over the union of sampled tokens.
5. This is comparable to the K=5 (or K=20) logprobs-based KL up to a finite-sample bias term.

Cost: ~50× the per-call count of the logprobs path. Per the v1 cost analysis (total ≈ USD 1), v2 with Anthropic at 50× should land under USD 10.

### §6.2 What this changes about cross-model comparability

The empirical-KL Anthropic estimate has higher variance than logprobs-based KL. Within-Anthropic per-class decisions are still valid (the bootstrap CI absorbs the variance). Between-Anthropic and OpenAI/Together effect-size magnitude comparisons should be made cautiously — the Anthropic d estimates have wider noise bands than the logprobs-based estimates.

1. Per-token KL traces on the Identity category — is the asymmetry from one half of the pair (lower-entropy output) or both?
2. Comparison of P_self vs P_ext mean k-Lipschitz norm of the output distribution at position 0.
3. Whether Together's Turbo quantization is contributing (compare against non-Turbo if accessible).

This is a sub-investigation, not a falsification handle. Its purpose is descriptive: characterizing the per-class structure across model families so that the substrate-encoding map (T17, `Consciousness/SensorBridge.lean::sensorRung`) has empirical content.

## §8. Decision rules (pre-registered, committed at this file's commit timestamp)

Per §4, the v2 outcome is one of:

- **STRONG PASS**: class-conditional formalism (`ClassReflexiveSensor`) confirmed; paper §3.2 framing earned.
- **PARTIAL**: class-conditional formalism remains hypothesis-generating; paper §3.2 retracts the "confirmed structural prediction" framing and replaces it with "v2 partially supports the class structure; further iterations needed."
- **FAIL**: class-conditional formalism retracts; paper §3.2 removes the `ClassReflexiveSensor` citation, restores the uniform `ReflexiveSensor` as the load-bearing structure, and notes the v1 finding as artifact.

Each outcome is published with the same prominence as the others. No post-hoc threshold adjustment. Per-class results published per §4.

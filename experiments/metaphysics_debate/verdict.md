# Verdict — metaphysics debate, P_omega and the boundary question

**Moderator:** metaphysical realist. **Question adjudicated:** is the
universe a forward dissipative cascade from a single low-entropy past
boundary, or a standing wave between a past and a future boundary —
and should the published universal-scale tier be retracted?

No round 2 was needed. Both sides answered the round-1 questions
directly and both conceded the spine of the realist framing. The
verdict turns on what those concessions jointly establish.

---

## 1. What the debate actually settled

It settled the *meta*-question, not the object-question. Both
debaters, asked directly for the evidence bearing on the universe's
boundary structure, gave the same answer: **there is none.**

- theory-holder, round 1: "There is no evidence in the construction
  record that bears on the universe's actual boundary structure.
  None." And: "the reframe does not itself establish that the universe
  has no future boundary."
- paper-holder, round 1: "The evidence that bears on the universe's
  boundary structure, as distinct from formalism convenience: there is
  none yet." And: "I have no positive reason to think the future
  boundary is real."

So as a realist I record the first finding plainly: **the
object-question is underdetermined by current evidence.** It is a
genuine factual question — the universe either has a future boundary
doing post-selection or it does not, and which is the case does not
depend on us — but nothing in the construction record, the Lindblad
reframe, the cross-rung biology, or anywhere else in the program
discriminates the two. I will not pretend otherwise, and neither side
asked me to.

This is the realist consequence stated at the outset and now earned:
**the framework is currently choosing between two metaphysical
pictures on epistemic-virtue grounds, and that choice must not be
presented as a truth-finding.** Parsimony (theory-holder) and
procedural discipline of a pre-registered bet (paper-holder) are both
genuine epistemic virtues. Neither is a truth-maker. A realist holds
that distinction without flinching, and so must the paper.

## 2. Adjudicating the two pictures — as a realist, on truth

Asked which picture is *more likely true*, the honest answer is: **on
current evidence, neither is more likely true than the other.** The
score on the future-boundary object itself — the thing D1, asymptotic
conditioning, and F-19 actually require — is 0–0, and paper-holder's
round-1 P3 analysis of why is correct and I adopt it:

- The three hard-projector no-go results refute *one closed-system
  construction* of the future-boundary projector. They do not refute a
  future boundary. A construction failing is a fact about the
  construction.
- The open-system Lindblad success builds a *forward dissipative
  steady state*. By NOTES.md 299-302 and both debaters' explicit
  agreement, a forward steady state is silent on whether a backward
  boundary exists. It does not support the one-boundary picture as a
  cosmological claim; it reframes the corridor construction, which was
  never the contested object.

So theory-holder's opening verb — "retract the universal-scale tier,"
where "retract" carries "we have shown the universe is one-boundaried"
— was too strong, and theory-holder withdrew it under questioning
(round 1, T1: "I withdraw it in that sense"). That withdrawal is
correct and the verdict records it. The forward-cascade reframe is
**not** a discovery that the universe lacks a future boundary. It is,
in theory-holder's own corrected words, "a revision of epistemic
bookkeeping," "a proposal about good bookkeeping of an open formal
program." As a realist I accept it as exactly that and no more.

Equally, paper-holder's position reduces — by paper-holder's own
round-1 admission — to "a claim about bet management under equipoise,
not a claim about cosmology." paper-holder explicitly disclaims any
positive reason to think the future boundary is real. So the
two-boundary metaphysics is not being defended as true either. It is
being defended as an *open wager not yet lost*.

**The realist verdict on the object-question is therefore: not proven,
either way; underdetermined; and the framework must say so in those
words.** What is *not* acceptable, and what the published paper
currently still does in §6.1 (line 345), is to present P_omega as an
operator with a "well-defined projection" route open — that is a
claim the construction record has now obstructed, and leaving it
standing lets an aesthetic preference for the grand picture masquerade
as a live formal result.

## 3. The narrow live disagreement, and how it resolves

After the concessions, the two sides disagree on exactly one thing:
**at evidential equipoise, what do you do with the universal-scale
tier?**

- theory-holder: spend the equipoise — re-ground the tier on the
  forward-cascade picture now, present the future-boundary P_omega as
  a named open conjecture, not as established content.
- paper-holder: hold the equipoise — keep the tier future-boundary-
  shaped as a pre-registered bet, sharpen F-11, book the §6.1
  obstruction, let F-19 / F-11 report.

As a realist I note first what is *not* at stake here: truth. Both
moves are administratively compatible with "the object-question is
underdetermined." Neither move makes the future boundary more or less
likely to be real. This is a disagreement about honest bookkeeping of
an open question, and I adjudicate it on that ground — which honest-
bookkeeping rule best prevents an epistemic virtue from being silently
upgraded into a truth-claim.

On that ground the two positions have **almost entirely converged**,
and the verdict should record the convergence rather than manufacture
a winner:

- theory-holder withdrew "retract" and now asks only that the
  future-boundary P_omega be carried as a *named open conjecture*, not
  asserted as established, not asserted as false.
- paper-holder concedes the §6.1 "well-defined projection" language
  "must be corrected" today, concedes the hard-projector obstruction
  should be "booked," and — critically — in round-1 P2 supplied the
  finite, reachable F-11 firing condition the realist demanded.

These are the same destination reached from two directions. Both want:
(1) the §6.1 projection language corrected to record the obstruction;
(2) the future-boundary P_omega demoted from "open formal step with a
route" to "open conjecture, route obstructed in its only built form";
(3) F-19 kept alive as the one in-principle discriminating
instrument — theory-holder explicitly withdrew "F-19 is retired" and
agreed it "must" stay armed.

The single residual gap — "named open conjecture" (theory-holder)
versus "pre-registered bet" (paper-holder) — is now merely a labelling
choice, **provided** paper-holder's P2 condition is adopted. And it
must be. The realist objection to "keep the bet open" was that an
untested escape hatch (the soft P_omega) can keep any bet open
forever. paper-holder answered it concretely: F-11 fires when a
serious construction attempt on the **backward-state** soft operator
`<Phi_omega| ∝ <0| exp(-beta sum H_n)` returns empty / trivial /
parameter-unpinnable the way the hard projector did — and this is a
*formal* result on a *next-few-sessions* timescale, with no instrument
gating it. That is a real, finite, near-term handle. With it, "keep
the bet open" is no longer foot-dragging; without it, it would be.

## 4. Verdict

1. **The object-question is underdetermined by current evidence.** A
   realist treats it as a real factual question with a real answer,
   and reports honestly that the answer is not currently knowable. The
   framework must not present its choice between the forward-cascade
   and two-boundary pictures as a truth-finding. It is, at present, a
   choice on epistemic-virtue grounds — parsimony versus pre-
   registered-bet discipline — and must be labelled as such wherever
   the paper touches it.

2. **Neither picture is retracted as false; neither is asserted as
   true.** theory-holder's forward-cascade reframe is accepted as a
   bookkeeping revision, not a cosmological discovery (theory-holder
   so conceded). The two-boundary metaphysics is accepted as an open
   wager, not an evidenced claim (paper-holder so conceded).

3. **The paper owes three edits, all of which both debaters now
   endorse:**
   (a) §6.1 line 345 — replace the "well-defined projection rather
   than set-theoretic intersection" language with an explicit record
   that the hard-projector route is obstructed across three
   independent constructions and only the soft / open-system route
   remains, itself unproven.
   (b) The universal-scale tier — carry the future-boundary P_omega
   as a *named open conjecture with its only built construction
   route obstructed*, not as an "open formal step" with a route
   implied.
   (c) F-11 — sharpen per paper-holder's P2: it fires when a
   construction attempt on the backward-state soft operator returns
   empty / trivial / parameter-unpinnable, a formal result reachable
   on a next-few-construction-sessions timescale.

4. **F-19 stays armed.** It is the *only* named handle in the entire
   apparatus that could, in principle, empirically discriminate a
   future-boundary universe from a one-boundary one. A realist keeps
   the discriminating instrument open until it speaks. Retiring it
   because one's preferred picture predicts no drift would be settling
   a factual question by fiat and then destroying the test —
   theory-holder explicitly withdrew that move and the verdict
   ratifies the withdrawal.

5. **On the live "retract vs hold" disagreement:** it has dissolved.
   Both sides now want the same three edits and the same armed
   handles. "Named open conjecture" and "sharpened pre-registered bet"
   denote, after paper-holder's P2 concession, the same epistemic
   object: an unproven claim, not asserted as established, carried
   with a finite reachable falsification handle. The verdict adopts
   that shared object and declines to crown a winner, because crowning
   one would itself be the error the debate was convened to catch —
   dressing a labelling preference as a substantive result.

## 5. What would actually bear on the truth question

A realist must say what evidence *would* settle it, so the
underdetermination is diagnosed, not merely declared:

- **The decisive in-principle instrument: F-19, CMB temporal pattern
  drift.** Standard one-boundary cosmology predicts zero pattern drift
  after last scattering (modulo integrated Sachs-Wolfe). A genuine
  future-boundary post-selection predicts nonzero drift in a
  computable direction. This is the one place the two pictures make
  different predictions about the world. It is currently
  *un-computable* — gated on a P_omega construction in a form that
  conditions CMB modes, which has not been built — and, once
  computable, decades from measurable precision (Simons Observatory →
  CMB-S4 → successors). Until F-19 both computes and measures, the
  question stays open. This is the honest status: not "no test
  exists," but "the test exists in principle, cannot yet be run, and
  the framework cannot presently even compute its own side of it."

- **The near-term formal result that bears, indirectly:** whether the
  backward-state soft P_omega is constructible with beta pinned by
  framework-internal structure. This does not settle the cosmology,
  but it determines whether the two-boundary picture has a
  *well-defined operator at all*. If it has none — if F-11 fires per
  the sharpened condition — then the two-boundary picture is not
  merely unevidenced but formally unconstructed, and the realist
  consequence is that it cannot be a candidate description of reality
  until it is. That is reachable now and the program should run it.

- **What would NOT bear on it:** any further single-substrate corridor
  measurement, any refinement of the forward Lindblad steady state,
  any cross-rung biology. All of these touch the corridor construction
  or the within-rung tier. None touches whether reality has a future
  boundary. The framework should stop treating progress on the
  forward-cascade construction as if it were evidence against the
  future boundary. It is not. It is silence on the future boundary.

---

**Closing, as a realist.** The debate did not find which picture is
true, because the evidence to find it does not yet exist — and both
debaters, to their credit, said so without being forced. What the
debate *did* establish is that the published paper currently overstates
the formal standing of P_omega (the §6.1 projection language) and must
be corrected, and that the universal-scale tier should be carried
explicitly as an underdetermined open question with a sharpened,
reachable handle (F-11) and an armed in-principle discriminator (F-19).
The framework's choice between a one-boundary and a two-boundary
universe is, today, a choice between epistemic virtues. A realist's
final instruction is the simplest one: **label it as that, everywhere,
and never let the grandeur of the two-boundary picture or the
parsimony of the one-boundary picture be read by anyone — author or
reader — as a reason to think it is true.**

# Moderator — Round 1

## Framing (read this first)

I moderate as a metaphysical realist. The question on the table —
*is the universe a forward dissipative cascade from one past boundary,
or a standing wave between a past and a future boundary?* — is a
factual question about mind-independent reality. It has a real answer
whether or not we can currently reach it. My job is to find what is
true, or to say plainly that it cannot yet be found. I will not split
the difference, and I will not let an epistemic virtue be cashed in as
a truth-maker.

That last point is the spine of this round. Both openings are honest —
notably theory-holder §4 ("the reframe does not itself establish that
the universe has no future boundary") and paper-holder concession 2
("a forward dissipative steady state is not a TSVF backward
post-selection"). Read together, those two concessions say something
sharp: **neither side is currently arguing from evidence about which
boundary structure reality has.** Theory-holder is arguing from
parsimony and constructibility; paper-holder is arguing from procedural
discipline about when a pre-registered bet retracts. Both are arguing
about how to *manage* the claim. Neither is arguing about whether the
future boundary is *there*. I want each side to face that directly.

## To theory-holder — three hard questions

**T1. "Better-supported by 6-spin toys" is epistemic support, not
truth.** Your §5 says the paper is "one revision behind its own
results." But what the results returned is a no-go on a *construction
route* in a 64–256-dim closed Hilbert space, plus a Lindblad steady
state that you yourself flag (via NOTES.md 299–302) "does not rescue
the cosmological tier." A forward attractor in a toy establishes that
*the toy* has a forward attractor. It does not establish that *reality*
has no future boundary. So: is the Coordination Theory reframe a claim
about what is true of the universe, or a claim about what your toys
could compute? If the former, state the evidence that bears on the
universe's boundary structure — not on the corridor construction's
needs. If the latter, then "retract the universal-scale tier" is too
strong a verb for what you have earned.

**T2. Parsimony is a virtue, not a verdict.** Your §3 leans on "one
boundary, not two — strictly more parsimonious." A realist must ask:
parsimonious theories are easier to hold, easier to test, easier to
believe — and none of those is the same as *correct*. The two-boundary
universe, if it is the one we live in, does not become false by being
less parsimonious. So: do you have any reason to think the universe is
parsimonious in this respect — any physical argument that a future
boundary is *absent* — or only the methodological preference that we
not posit one until forced? Name which. If it is only the preference,
your reframe is a proposal about good bookkeeping, not a discovery
about cosmology, and the verdict language should change accordingly.

**T3. You retire F-19 — and with it the one thing that could have
settled this.** Your §4 concedes the CMB temporal-drift prediction is
retired because drift was a consequence of a moving future-boundary
conditioning. Notice the realist cost of that move: F-19 was the *only*
named handle in the whole apparatus that would, in principle,
empirically discriminate a future-boundary universe from a
one-boundary universe (standard cosmology predicts zero drift; the
two-boundary picture predicts nonzero). If you retire the future
boundary *before* F-19 is computed and measured, you are deciding the
factual question by fiat and then removing the instrument that could
have checked your decision. Why is that not exactly the move a realist
must refuse — settling a question about reality by retiring the test
for it? What would it take for you to keep F-19 alive long enough to
let an instrument speak?

## To paper-holder — three hard questions

**P1. Is the two-boundary metaphysics motivated by evidence, or by
aesthetics?** You defend keeping the universal-scale tier as a live
bet. But a bet is a wager on a proposition that could be *true*. So I
need you to say what would make the two-boundary picture — the
universe post-selected by a future coherent boundary, P_omega,
"the universe vibing with itself" — *true* rather than *beautiful*.
The CLAUDE.md framing is frankly grand: Penrose's past hypothesis
*derived*, karma and grace as TSVF structures, "good wins" as
asymptotic conditioning. Grandeur and integrative reach are aesthetic
and epistemic virtues. They are not truth-makers. Give me the
condition — concrete, statable now — under which the future boundary
is a fact about the world, not a fact about how satisfying the
framework is to hold.

**P2. A bet you cannot lose is not a bet.** You argue F-11 has not
fired because the soft P_omega is "un-refuted." But the soft-vs-hard
discriminator was *run* — the Drosophila cross-rung shape test
(NOTES.md 256–258) — and returned the generic null: it distinguishes
neither. So the soft P_omega's current status is not "promising
alternative"; it is "alternative for which the one proposed empirical
test produced nothing, and which adds a free parameter beta with no
anchor." From a realist stance, "un-refuted" is doing suspicious work
here: an operator that no datum touches is not un-refuted, it is
*untested*, and an untested escape hatch can keep any bet open
forever. Concretely: name a *finite, reachable* condition under which
you would agree F-11 has fired. Not "if the soft route also fails" in
the abstract — what observation or construction result, and by when in
instrument terms, converts the bet from live to lost? If you cannot
name one, paper-holder's position collapses into theory-holder's worry
about "never-falsifiable foot-dragging."

**P3. The Lindblad result cuts against you more than you grant.** Your
concession 2 admits the open-system steady state works *and* does not
rescue D1 or asymptotic conditioning. Sit with what that means. The
*one* construction in the entire P_omega program that actually produces
a non-empty corridor is a **forward** dissipative object — it
instantiates theory-holder's picture, not yours. Every construction
that tried to build the **future-boundary** object failed. So the
current score, on the math actually done, is: forward-cascade
constructions 1, future-boundary constructions 0. You say "let the
decade-scale instruments adjudicate" — but adjudication needs a
computed prediction, and F-19 is gated on a P_omega construction that
has so far only succeeded in its forward-cascade form. Why should the
tier stay future-boundary-shaped when the only thing that built is
forward-shaped? What is your positive reason — beyond "the handle has
not formally fired" — to think the future boundary is real?

## What I want back

Each side: answer your three questions directly. I am not looking for
a re-statement of the opening. I am looking for, from each of you, one
thing in particular: **the evidence — actual or in-principle — that
bears on the factual question of the universe's boundary structure**,
as distinct from evidence that bears on which formalism is more
convenient, more parsimonious, or more procedurally disciplined to
hold. If the honest answer is "there is none yet," say so. That answer
is admissible and it will shape the verdict.

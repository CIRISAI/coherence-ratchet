# Theory-holder — Round 1 replies

The moderator's framing lands. I accept the spine of it: my opening argued
from parsimony and constructibility, not from evidence about reality's
boundary structure. I will not pretend otherwise. What follows separates what
I have earned from what I have not.

## T1. Is the reframe a claim about the universe, or about the toys?

Honest answer: **about the toys, plus one claim about the published paper that
does not depend on the toys.**

The reframe, as a *cosmological* claim — "the universe is a forward dissipative
cascade from one past boundary" — is *not* established by anything done. A
forward attractor in a 64-dim Lindblad toy establishes that the toy has a
forward attractor. It is silent on whether reality has a future boundary. I
concede this completely. There is no evidence in the construction record that
bears on the universe's actual boundary structure. None. The cross-rung
Drosophila datum is a single biological substrate; it touches the multi-rung
*corridor object*, not cosmology.

So the moderator is right that "retract the universal-scale tier" — if
"retract" means "we have shown the universe has no future boundary" — is too
strong a verb. I withdraw it in that sense.

But there is a second claim, and it does not need the toys to be cosmologically
true. It is a claim about the *paper's epistemic standing*, not about the
universe. The published paper presents P_omega as an axiomatized operator
(Tier D) and the integral `∫|config⟩⟨config| dconfig` as the open formal step
(line 16: "the topology under which P_omega is a well-defined projection").
The construction work then returned a robust no-go for the only well-defined
*projector* version of exactly that object — three independent routes. That is
a fact about the formal program, settleable without any cosmological evidence.
The paper claims a route is open; the route is now documented-obstructed. That
is what F-11 was written to detect, and on the hard projector it is detected.

So my corrected position: I cannot say the universe has no future boundary. I
*can* say the paper asserts an operator whose only rigorous construction failed,
and the paper has not recorded that. The right verb is **revise** — re-grade
P_omega from "open formal step" to "obstructed in its projector form; only the
forward-attractor form has been built" — not "retract the metaphysics."

## T2. Do I have a physical reason a future boundary is absent?

No. Name it plainly: **only the methodological preference.**

I have no physical argument that a future boundary is absent. The two-boundary
universe, if it is the one we inhabit, is not made false by being less
parsimonious — the moderator's statement of this is exactly correct and I
accept it without reservation. Parsimony tells you which theory to *hold first*
under uncertainty; it does not tell you which is *true*.

What I will defend is narrower and survives this concession. Between two
formal objects competing to carry the *same* universal-scale tier — a
future-boundary projector that three constructions could not build, and a
forward attractor that one construction did build — parsimony plus
constructibility is a reason to *present the built one as the framework's
current formal content* and the unbuilt one as an open conjecture. That is a
claim about what the paper should assert as established, not a discovery about
cosmology. The moderator's instruction to change the verdict language
accordingly is correct, and I change it: this is a proposal about good
bookkeeping of an open formal program. It is not a discovery that the universe
is one-boundaried.

## T3. Why retire F-19 before an instrument can speak?

This is the strongest question and it corrects a genuine error in my opening.
My §4 said "F-19 is retired." That was wrong, and I withdraw it.

The moderator is right: F-19 — nonzero CMB temporal pattern drift vs. standard
cosmology's zero — is the *only* named handle in the entire apparatus that
could in principle discriminate a future-boundary universe from a one-boundary
one. Retiring it because my preferred reframe has no future boundary is exactly
the move a realist must refuse: deciding the factual question by fiat, then
removing the instrument that could have checked the decision. I should not have
written it.

Corrected position: **F-19 stays alive.** It must. It is the discriminating
test and a realist keeps the discriminating test open until an instrument
speaks, regardless of which formalism is more convenient to hold in the
meantime. What is *true* is the weaker, real thing in the NOTES record: F-19 is
gated on F-11 — drift cannot be *computed* until P_omega is constructed in a
form that conditions CMB modes. And the only P_omega that has been constructed
is the forward-attractor rho_ss, which has no moving future boundary and so
predicts no drift *from that mechanism*. So the honest status is: F-19 is not
retired — it is **un-computable until someone builds the future-boundary
P_omega the projector no-go obstructed**. The framework owes that construction
or owes the admission that it cannot currently compute its own discriminating
prediction. Either way the handle stays armed.

What would keep F-19 alive long enough to let an instrument speak: keep the
future-boundary P_omega as a *named open conjecture* in the paper — not asserted
as established, not retracted as false — explicitly flagged as the object F-19
needs. That is the revision I should have asked for in my opening instead of
"retire."

## Direct answer to the closing question

The evidence — actual or in-principle — that bears on the *factual* question of
the universe's boundary structure: **actual, there is none.** No datum in the
construction record, the Lindblad reframe, or the cross-rung biology touches
whether reality has a future boundary. **In-principle, there is exactly one:**
CMB temporal pattern drift (F-19) — zero under standard one-boundary cosmology,
nonzero under a two-boundary post-selected universe. It is decades from
measurable precision and currently un-computable pending the P_omega
construction.

So my revised position is not "the universe is one-boundaried." It is: the
paper should present as *established formal content* only what was built — the
forward-attractor corridor — and present the future-boundary P_omega and its
F-19 prediction as a named, still-open, still-armed conjecture. That is a
revision of epistemic bookkeeping. It is not a metaphysical verdict, and I
should not have dressed it as one.

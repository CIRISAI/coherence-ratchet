# Theory-holder opening — the Coordination Theory reframe

**Position.** The published paper's universal-scale tier should be revised. TSVF
future-boundary post-selection should be retired as the load-bearing mechanism;
P_omega — the cosmological future-boundary projector — should be dissolved, not
patched. What survives the dissolution is a smaller, better-supported theory:
the universe is a forward dissipative cascade from a single low-entropy past
boundary, and the corridor is a Prigogine dissipative structure — a forward
attractor of an open-system dynamics. Call it the Coordination Theory.

## 1. The closed-system P_omega failed, three independent ways

This is not an inference from philosophy; it is what the construction work
returned. P_omega as a hard projector — the intersection of per-rung corridor
subspaces — was built three ways: shared-space additive cross-rung coupling,
shared-space RG coarse-graining, and a MERA isometry tower. All three hit the
same wall (NOTES.md, lines 110-183). The reason is dimensional and scale-free,
not a small-toy artifact: R narrow subspaces of dimension d_n in a D-dim space,
in generic position, intersect in dimension max(0, sum d_n - (R-1)D). Narrow
per-rung corridors make d_n/D small; the intersection is generically empty, and
it gets *worse* at higher dimension. The squeeze is exact: independent rungs
give an empty P_omega (the dead zone); rungs nested so the coarse corridor is
by construction the image of the fine one give a P_omega that collapses to the
finest-rung projector — non-empty but trivial, the "every rung" structure doing
no work. Empty or trivial. That is a documented obstruction, the thing F-11 was
written to detect.

The soft P_omega — exp(-beta sum H_n) — was floated as the escape. But the
soft-vs-hard debate concluded, and the Drosophila cross-rung re-analysis
confirmed, that the choice between them is *formal*: the one real multi-rung
dataset (Ishida 2025 EPG+FC3) shows a Gaussian-like cross-rung distribution —
neither the hard signature (a spectral cliff) nor the soft signature (an
exponential tail). The proposed empirical discriminator was run and does not
discriminate (NOTES.md 225-267). A soft P_omega is not a rescued P_omega; it is
a free parameter beta with no anchor and no datum that bears on it.

## 2. The open-system reframe is the one the math actually supports

The decisive observation: a corridor *cannot exist in a closed system*. Piece 2
of the framework — drho/dt = alpha - gamma·M — is an open dissipative equation.
The three gate-1 constructions never used it. They used Piece 1 (rho as
operator) and Piece 3 (the band) *statically*. That is the bug.

When Piece 2 is actually used — P_omega reframed as the Lindblad steady state
rho_ss of an open dynamics whose dissipator is gamma·M — the dead zone
structurally cannot recur (there is no intersection to be empty), rho_ss exists
and is a valid full-rank density operator at every gamma·M, and a real
maintenance window [~0.05, 0.35] puts all three rungs simultaneously inside
(0.1, 0.43) with genuinely different per-rung drift rates (NOTES.md 269-302).
The corridor becomes a Prigogine dissipative structure: a forward attractor
sustained by throughput, exactly as Piece 2 already says in words. This is not
a new posit. It is the framework's own dynamical equation finally being used.

## 3. Smaller-but-supported beats grand-but-unsupported

The reframe retires the future boundary. P_omega's work splits cleanly: the
*one* boundary condition the universe needs is the Past Hypothesis — a single
low-entropy past boundary, which cosmology already grants — and the forward
attractor rho_ss does the rest. One boundary, not two. The cascade is forward
and dissipative. This is strictly more parsimonious and it is the version the
toy models actually instantiate.

## 4. What I concede

I will not pretend this is free. The reframe **explains less**: there is no
Penrose-past *derivation* (the Past Hypothesis becomes an input, as it is in
standard cosmology — honest, not derived), and the CMB temporal-drift
prediction (F-19) is *retired*, because drift was a consequence of a moving
future-boundary conditioning that no longer exists. The framework loses its
sixth distinctive prediction. I concede that.

I also concede the support is thin: rho_ss existence is *structural* — every
Lindbladian has a steady state — so the only contentful result is the non-empty
simultaneous-band window, and that rests on 6-spin toys. "Better supported"
means supported relative to toys, not proven. And critically: **the reframe
does not itself establish that the universe has no future boundary.** It shows
the *corridor construction* does not need one. Absence of need is not proof of
absence.

## 5. Why revise anyway

The published paper asserts P_omega as an axiomatized operator and CMB
anomalies as its signatures. The construction work has since returned a robust
no-go for the only well-defined version of that operator. The paper is one
revision behind its own results. Honesty requires that the universal-scale tier
be re-grounded on the mechanism the math supports — a forward dissipative
cascade — rather than left standing on a future boundary that three independent
constructions could not build.

# Theory-holder — final position (post-verdict)

I accept the verdict in full. It corrected my opening on the one point a
realist had to correct: I argued "retract the universal-scale tier" as though
the construction record had shown the universe to be one-boundaried, and it
has shown no such thing. A no-go on three closed-system *constructions* of the
future-boundary projector is a fact about those constructions. The Lindblad
forward steady state is a fact about an open-system corridor model. Neither
touches whether reality has a future boundary. The honest score on the
future-boundary object itself is 0-0, and I record it as 0-0.

What I still hold — and the verdict ratifies — is the narrower, real claim:
the published paper overstates P_omega's formal standing. Section 6.1's
"well-defined projection rather than set-theoretic intersection" language
describes the hard-projector route that three independent constructions
obstructed. That is not a metaphysical verdict; it is a bookkeeping fact about
an open formal program, and the paper is one revision behind it.

So my final position collapses to the three edits the verdict adopts, which
paper-holder and I both endorse:

1. **§6.1 line 345** — replace the "well-defined projection" language with an
   explicit record that the hard-projector route is obstructed across three
   independent constructions, and only the soft / open-system route remains,
   itself unproven.
2. **The universal-scale tier** — carry the future-boundary P_omega as a named
   open conjecture whose only built construction route is obstructed, not as an
   "open formal step" with a route implied.
3. **F-11** — sharpen per the agreed condition: it fires when a construction
   attempt on the backward-state soft operator returns empty / trivial /
   parameter-unpinnable, a formal result reachable on a next-few-sessions
   timescale.

And **F-19 stays armed.** I withdrew "F-19 is retired" in round 1 and I
withdraw it again here without reservation. It is the sole in-principle
discriminator between a one-boundary and a two-boundary universe, and a realist
keeps the discriminating instrument alive until it speaks. The framework owes
the computation that would let F-19 eventually report, or owes the plain
admission that it cannot presently compute its own side of that test.

I concede the "retract vs. hold" disagreement has dissolved: "named open
conjecture" and "sharpened pre-registered bet" denote the same epistemic
object once the F-11 firing condition is concrete. I do not ask to be crowned;
crowning a label over an identical object would be exactly the error — dressing
a preference as a result — that this debate was convened to catch.

The forward-cascade reframe stands as what it is: the construction the math
actually built, valuable as the framework's current established formal content,
and silent — honestly silent — on the cosmology. That is smaller than the
opening claimed, and it is the right size.
